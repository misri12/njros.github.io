# THE NATIONAL JIRGA REGISTRATION AND OVERSIGHT ACT, 2026

---

**A Bill**  
*to provide for the mandatory registration, digital oversight, and lawful conduct of jirgas; to establish the National Jirga Monitoring Authority; to require reporting of criminal matters to police; and for matters connected therewith or incidental thereto*

---

WHEREAS jirgas and analogous customary councils have historically served communities in dispute resolution throughout Pakistan;

AND WHEREAS the unregulated conduct of jirgas has in numerous instances resulted in decisions contrary to the Constitution and the law, including in matters involving sexual offences, homicide, forced marriage, Swara, Vani, denial of inheritance, and unlawful deprivation of property;

AND WHEREAS it is expedient to regulate jirgas through mandatory national registration, digital record-keeping, police notification, and independent oversight, without abolishing lawful customary dispute resolution;

It is hereby enacted as follows:—

---

## CHAPTER I  
## PRELIMINARY

**1. Short title, extent and commencement.—** (1) This Act may be called the **National Jirga Registration and Oversight Act, 2026**.

(2) It extends to the whole of Pakistan.

(3) It shall come into force at once, except that—

(a) Chapter III, Chapter IV, Chapter V, Chapter VI, Chapter VII, Chapter VIII, Chapter IX and Chapter XII shall come into force on such date as the Federal Government may, by notification in the official Gazette, appoint; and

(b) different dates may be appointed for different provisions or for different areas.

**2. Application.—** (1) This Act shall apply to every jirga and to every person who convenes, constitutes, participates in, records, enforces, or otherwise facilitates the proceedings or decisions of a jirga.

(2) Nothing in this Act shall be construed to—

(a) abolish the institution of jirga where conducted in accordance with this Act; or

(b) affect the jurisdiction of courts of law established under the Constitution.

**3. Act to override other laws.—** The provisions of this Act shall have effect notwithstanding anything to the contrary contained in any custom, usage, tradition, riwaj, Pashtunwali, tribal practice, or any other law for the time being in force, except the Constitution.

**4. Purpose and objects.—** The objects of this Act are to—

(a) ensure mandatory registration of every jirga through the official digital portal established under this Act;

(b) prohibit jirga proceedings and decisions on matters forbidden by law;

(c) secure transparency, accountability, and digital record-keeping of lawful jirga proceedings;

(d) ensure mandatory reporting to police of cognizable criminal matters;

(e) protect fundamental rights, particularly of women, children, and vulnerable persons; and

(f) establish an independent oversight authority.

---

## CHAPTER II  
## DEFINITIONS

**5. Definitions.—** In this Act, unless there is anything repugnant in the subject or context,—

(a) **"Authority"** means the National Jirga Monitoring Authority established under section 58;

(b) **"case"** means a dispute or matter brought before a registered jirga and assigned a unique case identification number on the Portal;

(c) **"Chairperson"** means the Chairperson of the Authority;

(d) **"Code of Conduct"** means the code of conduct prescribed under section 24;

(e) **"cognizable offence"** means a cognizable offence within the meaning of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898 (Act V of 1898);

(f) **"convener"** means a person who calls, organizes, or presides over a jirga;

(g) **"decision"** means any finding, direction, order, award, settlement, sulh, faisla, or other determination made by a jirga in respect of a case;

(h) **"decision record"** means a decision recorded and submitted on the Portal in accordance with section 52;

(i) **"District Monitoring Officer"** means an officer appointed under section 65;

(j) **"illegal jirga"** means—

(i) a jirga that is not registered under this Act;

(ii) a jirga that conducts any proceeding or issues any decision otherwise than through the Portal;

(iii) a registered jirga conducting proceedings while its registration is suspended or expired; or

(iv) a gathering styled by any other name which, in substance, functions as a jirga within the meaning of this Act;

(k) **"jirga"** means any assembly, council, meeting, or body of elders, notables, religious figures, tribal leaders, or community representatives, by whatever name called, convened for the purpose of hearing, discussing, mediating, arbitrating, or deciding a dispute or matter affecting rights, obligations, status, property, honour, marriage, inheritance, or personal security of any person or persons, and includes a panchayat, jirga-e-baloch, faislo council, hujra council, or analogous customary body exercising binding or coercive social authority;

(l) **"jirga member"** means a person registered as a member of a registered jirga under section 19;

(m) **"Jirga Member Identification Number" or "JMID"** means the unique identification number assigned to a jirga member on the Portal;

(n) **"Jirga Registration Number" or "JRN"** means the unique registration number assigned to a registered jirga on the Portal;

(o) **"member"** means a jirga member;

(p) **"minor"** means a person who has not attained the age of eighteen years;

(q) **"party"** means a complainant, respondent, victim, heir, witness, or any other person whose rights or interests are directly affected by a case before a jirga;

(r) **"person"** includes any natural person and, where the context so requires, a jirga as a collective body;

(s) **"police"** means the police force established under any law for the time being in force and includes an officer empowered to investigate cognizable offences;

(t) **"Portal"** means the official digital registration and oversight portal established under section 26;

(u) **"prescribed"** means prescribed by rules made under this Act;

(v) **"proceeding"** means any meeting, hearing, deliberation, consultation, or other step taken by a jirga in connection with a case, whether preliminary, interim, or final;

(w) **"proceedings summary"** means a summary of a hearing or meeting recorded on the Portal under section 51;

(x) **"registered jirga"** means a jirga registered under Chapter III and holding a valid JRN;

(y) **"registration"** means registration of a jirga on the Portal under this Act;

(z) **"rules"** means rules made under section 75;

(za) **"Schedule"** means a Schedule to this Act;

(zb) **"secretary"** means a person designated by a registered jirga to file cases, proceedings, and decisions on the Portal;

(zc) **"Swara"** includes Vani, Sang Chatti, and any practice whereby a woman or girl is given or promised in marriage, whether to a person or family, as compensation, punishment, settlement, or dispute resolution for an offence, injury, elopement, or dispute committed or alleged to have been committed by another person;

(zd) **"victim"** means a person who has suffered harm, injury, loss, or threat to life, liberty, body, property, or dignity in connection with a matter before a jirga or an illegal jirga.

## CHAPTER III  
## MANDATORY REGISTRATION OF EVERY JIRGA

**6. Mandatory registration.—** (1) No jirga shall be convened, constituted, or permitted to conduct any proceeding or issue any decision unless it is registered under this Act and unless such proceeding or decision is conducted and recorded exclusively through the Portal.

(2) Registration under this Act shall be mandatory and shall not be optional, discretionary, or waivable by any person, custom, or authority.

(3) Any jirga conducted outside the official registration Portal, or without a valid JRN, shall be deemed an **illegal jirga** for the purposes of this Act.

**7. Prohibition on proceedings by unregistered jirgas.—** (1) No person shall convene, participate in as a member, secretarially assist, attend in an official capacity, enforce, or give effect to the proceeding or decision of an illegal jirga.

(2) A decision of an illegal jirga shall be void and shall not be recognized, enforced, or given effect to by any court, police officer, public authority, or person.

(3) No public servant shall treat an illegal jirga decision as a lawful settlement of any matter, including for the purpose of refusing registration of an FIR or closing a criminal investigation.

**8. Application for registration.—** (1) A person seeking to constitute a jirga shall apply for registration on the Portal in the prescribed form.

(2) An application shall include—

(a) the proposed name of the jirga;

(b) the tier sought under section 14;

(c) the proposed geographic jurisdiction;

(d) the list of proposed members with their CNIC numbers and contact details;

(e) designation of a secretary;

(f) adoption of the Code of Conduct;

(g) community endorsements as prescribed; and

(h) such other particulars as may be prescribed.

**9. Grant or refusal of registration.—** (1) The District Monitoring Officer shall, after verification under section 20 and subject to the prescribed procedure, grant or refuse registration within twenty-one working days of receipt of a complete application.

(2) Where registration is granted, the Portal shall issue a JRN and a digital registration certificate.

(3) Where registration is refused, the District Monitoring Officer shall record reasons in writing and communicate them to the applicant through the Portal.

(4) Failure to pass an order within the period specified in sub-section (1) shall not confer registration by deemed approval unless otherwise prescribed.

**10. Validity and renewal of registration.—** (1) Registration of a jirga shall remain valid for one year from the date of grant unless suspended or cancelled earlier.

(2) A registered jirga shall apply for renewal on the Portal before expiry of its registration.

(3) A jirga whose registration has expired and has not been renewed shall be treated as an illegal jirga until renewal is granted.

**11. Amendment of registration.—** (1) A registered jirga shall notify any change in membership, jurisdiction, secretary, or tier through the Portal within seven days of such change.

(2) Material changes in membership or jurisdiction shall require approval of the District Monitoring Officer.

**12. Suspension and cancellation of registration.—** (1) The Authority or a District Monitoring Officer may, by order in writing and after affording an opportunity of hearing, suspend or cancel the registration of a jirga on any ground specified in section 57 or section 56.

(2) During suspension, the jirga shall be deemed an illegal jirga for the duration of suspension.

(3) Cancellation shall permanently revoke the JRN unless otherwise ordered on appeal.

**13. Case filing prerequisite.—** (1) No registered jirga shall commence any proceeding in respect of any case unless the case has been opened and assigned a case identification number on the Portal.

(2) Opening a case on the Portal shall be mandatory before any meeting, hearing, or deliberation relating to that matter.

(3) Any proceeding conducted before case opening on the Portal shall constitute conduct of an illegal jirga.

**14. Tiers of registration.—** (1) Registration shall be granted in one of the following tiers:

(a) **Tier I**—community or village level jirga;

(b) **Tier II**—tehsil or multi-village level jirga; or

(c) **Tier III**—district or inter-tribal level jirga.

(2) The prescribed criteria for each tier shall be specified in the rules.

**15. Public verification.—** (1) The registration status of a jirga may be verified on the Portal through its JRN or QR code.

(2) A person dealing with a jirga may rely on the Portal to ascertain whether the jirga is registered, suspended, or expired.

**16. Duty to report illegal jirgas.—** (1) Any person having knowledge of an illegal jirga may report the same on the Portal, to the Authority, to police, or to the District Monitoring Officer.

(2) No person making a report in good faith under this section shall be subject to civil or criminal liability.

## CHAPTER IV  
## DIGITAL REGISTRATION PORTAL

**17. Establishment of Portal.—** (1) The Federal Government shall establish and maintain an official digital registration and oversight portal to be known as the **National Jirga Registration and Oversight System Portal** or **NJROS Portal**.

(2) The Portal shall be the sole official platform for—

(a) registration, renewal, amendment, suspension, and cancellation of jirgas;

(b) registration of jirga members;

(c) opening and management of cases;

(d) recording of proceedings summaries and decision records;

(e) police notifications and alerts;

(f) appeals and administrative actions under this Act; and

(g) public verification and prescribed statistical disclosure.

**18. Exclusive use of Portal.—** (1) No registration, case, proceeding, decision, appeal, or official communication required under this Act shall be valid unless effected through the Portal:

*Provided that* where connectivity is unavailable, offline capture may be permitted in the prescribed manner, but final submission to the Portal shall be mandatory within the prescribed time.

(2) Any jirga proceeding conducted, or decision recorded, outside the Portal—whether in writing, orally, through messaging applications, paper register, or any other medium—shall constitute an illegal jirga and shall be void.

(3) Manual, parallel, or private registers maintained by a jirga shall not substitute for the Portal.

**19. Registration of members on Portal.—** (1) Every member of a registered jirga shall be registered on the Portal and shall be assigned a JMID.

(2) No person shall act as a member of a registered jirga unless he holds a valid JMID linked to the relevant JRN.

(3) A member shall be disqualified from registration where he falls within section 22.

**20. Verification through Portal.—** (1) The Portal shall verify identity of applicants and members through integration with the National Database and Registration Authority or other prescribed systems.

(2) The Portal may access prescribed criminal justice databases for vetting and risk assessment.

**21. Accessibility and offline access.—** (1) The Federal Government shall ensure that the Portal is accessible in Urdu and English at minimum, and in such other languages as may be prescribed.

(2) The Authority shall establish field facilitation centers where prescribed for applicants and secretaries unable to access the Portal directly.

(3) Rules may provide for SMS, USSD, or other low-bandwidth interfaces.

**22. Eligibility of members.—** (1) A person shall not be registered as a jirga member if he—

(a) is not a citizen of Pakistan;

(b) is under thirty-five years of age, unless permitted under rules;

(c) has been convicted of an offence specified in Schedule I or of an offence under this Act;

(d) has been convicted under the Prevention of Anti-Women Practices (Criminal Law Amendment) Act, 2011 (Act VIII of 2011);

(e) is an undischarged insolvent; or

(f) has been permanently debarred by the Authority.

(2) A member shall complete prescribed training before participating in any case.

**23. Security and data protection.—** (1) The Portal shall maintain appropriate cybersecurity, encryption, audit trails, and access controls.

(2) Personal data on the Portal shall be processed in accordance with the law for the time being in force relating to data protection.

(3) Unauthorized access to or disclosure of Portal data shall be punishable under this Act and other applicable law.

**24. Code of Conduct.—** (1) The Authority shall prescribe a Code of Conduct for registered jirgas and members.

(2) Every registered jirga and member shall adhere to the Code of Conduct.

(3) Breach of the Code of Conduct may attract administrative penalties under Chapter IX.

**25. Rules for Portal operation.—** The Authority may, with the approval of the Federal Government, prescribe rules for the operation, workflow, authentication, and technical standards of the Portal.

## CHAPTER V  
## REGISTRATION REQUIREMENTS

**26. Portal under control of Authority.—** For the purposes of this Chapter and Chapter IV, the Portal shall function under the administrative control and supervision of the Authority.

**27. Documents and endorsements.—** (1) An application for registration shall be accompanied by—

(a) a charter or constitution of the jirga adopting the Code of Conduct;

(b) prescribed community endorsements;

(c) jurisdiction map or revenue estate details; and

(d) oath of compliance by each proposed member.

(2) The District Monitoring Officer may require additional information or field verification before granting Tier II or Tier III registration.

**28. Secretary of jirga.—** (1) Every registered jirga shall designate a secretary responsible for Portal filings.

(2) The secretary shall be literate and trained in the prescribed manner.

(3) Failure by the secretary to file required records shall constitute a contravention of this Act by the jirga and the secretary.

**29. Quorum and attendance.—** (1) No decision shall be recorded on the Portal unless at least sixty per cent of the registered members of the jirga are marked present through the Portal.

(2) Party attendance shall be recorded through CNIC verification or prescribed exception approved by the District Monitoring Officer.

**30. Party identification.—** (1) Every party to a case shall be identified on the Portal through CNIC or prescribed alternative for exceptional cases.

(2) Where a party is a minor or lacks legal capacity, the Portal shall record lawful guardian or representative details.

**31. Classification of cases.—** (1) The secretary shall classify each case on the Portal according to the prescribed categories.

(2) Misclassification of a case, where proved deliberate, shall constitute an offence under this Act.

**32. Cooling-off periods.—** (1) The rules may prescribe mandatory cooling-off periods before recording decisions in sensitive categories, including inheritance and betrothal disputes.

(2) The Portal shall block decision submission until prescribed cooling-off periods have elapsed.

**33. Fees.—** (1) The Federal Government may, by notification in the official Gazette, prescribe registration, renewal, and case filing fees.

(2) Fee waivers may be prescribed for indigent parties or during pilot phases.

**34. Registration appeals.—** (1) An applicant aggrieved by refusal of registration may appeal to the provincial director of the Authority within fifteen days.

(2) An order on appeal shall be passed within thirty days.

## CHAPTER VI  
## PROHIBITED MATTERS

**35. Matters prohibited before jirgas.—** (1) Notwithstanding any custom or practice, no jirga, whether registered or illegal, shall hear, mediate, decide, facilitate, recommend, or give effect to any matter prohibited under this Act.

(2) A registered jirga shall not record on the Portal, and the Portal shall block, any proceeding or decision relating to a prohibited matter.

**36. Prohibited decisions.—** No jirga shall order, recommend, facilitate, or give effect to—

(a) Swara, Vani, or marriage of a woman or girl as compensation, punishment, or dispute resolution;

(b) forced marriage or marriage contrary to the free consent of a party;

(c) Ghag or declaration of betrothal without consent of the woman;

(d) compulsion upon any person to withdraw a criminal complaint or refrain from reporting an offence to police;

(e) settlement, compromise, or forgiveness in respect of a non-compoundable offence, except through a court of competent jurisdiction;

(f) settlement of rape, gang rape, sexual abuse, or offence under sections 375 or 376 of the Pakistan Penal Code, 1860 (Act XLV of 1860);

(g) denial of inheritance rights contrary to the Muslim Family Laws Ordinance, 1961 (VIII of 1961) and the West Pakistan Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act, 1962 (V of 1962);

(h) deprivation of property through coercion or without lawful authority;

(i) expulsion from homeland, social boycott, or intimidation intended to obstruct access to justice;

(j) custody or marriage of a minor contrary to law;

(k) any matter exclusively reserved to a court of law under any law for the time being in force; or

(l) any act or omission declared prohibited in Schedule II.

**37. Criminal matters.—** (1) Where a case involves an offence specified in Schedule I or any cognizable offence, the registered jirga shall not finalize any decision on the Portal except as permitted under section 45 and the rules.

(2) The Portal shall automatically lock decision recording in respect of such cases upon risk flagging or police notification.

(3) Continuation of jirga proceedings after lock without lawful unlock shall constitute conduct of an illegal jirga.

**38. Vulnerable parties.—** (1) In any case involving a woman, child, person with disability, or person in a manifestly coercive situation, the jirga shall ensure that proceedings on the Portal record vulnerability tags and trigger prescribed protection measures.

(2) No decision affecting a vulnerable party shall be valid unless prescribed safeguards have been complied with.

**39. Void decisions.—** (1) A decision of a jirga on a prohibited matter shall be void *ab initio*.

(2) A void decision shall not confer rights, extinguish liabilities, or bar prosecution or civil remedies.

(3) Any person enforcing or attempting to enforce a void decision shall be liable under this Act.

**40. Superiority of law.—** (1) No decision of a registered jirga shall override the Constitution or any valid law.

(2) In case of conflict between a lawful jirga decision and statute, the statute shall prevail.

**41. Islamic sulh.—** Nothing in this Chapter shall prohibit a lawful sulh or compromise in respect of a compoundable matter where permitted by law and approved by a court of competent jurisdiction where such approval is required.

## CHAPTER VII  
## MANDATORY POLICE REPORTING

**42. Duty to report criminal matters.—** (1) Where a case before a registered jirga involves or discloses a cognizable offence, the Portal shall generate a mandatory police alert.

(2) The secretary, convener, and every member present shall be under a continuing duty to ensure that the matter is reported on the Portal and to police without delay.

(3) Concealment, minimization, or deliberate misclassification of a criminal matter shall constitute an offence under this Act.

**43. Automatic notification.—** (1) The Portal shall transmit alerts to the prescribed police dashboard, District Police Officer, and National Jirga Police Liaison Officer for the district.

(2) Alerts shall include case details, JRN, party information, recommended offences, risk score, and timestamps.

(3) Priority alerts shall be transmitted in respect of offences involving sexual violence, homicide, child abuse, kidnapping, acid throwing, and offences listed in Part A of Schedule I.

**44. Police response.—** (1) The prescribed police officer shall acknowledge an alert on the Portal within the prescribed time.

(2) Where a cognizable offence is disclosed, police shall proceed in accordance with the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898 (Act V of 1898).

(3) The FIR number, where registered, shall be entered on the Portal within twenty-four hours.

**45. Effect of police notification.—** (1) Upon generation of a priority alert or registration of an FIR, the Portal shall suspend decision recording in the case unless and until unlocked in the prescribed manner through upload of a court order or prosecutor direction.

(2) No jirga decision recorded in violation of sub-section (1) shall have any legal effect.

**46. Duty of members upon discovery of offence.—** (1) If a cognizable offence arises or is disclosed during a proceeding, the jirga shall immediately adjourn and ensure Portal reporting and police notification before any further proceeding.

(2) Failure to adjourn and report shall constitute an offence under section 48.

**47. Whistleblower protection.—** (1) A person reporting an illegal jirga or criminal concealment in good faith shall be protected from retaliation.

(2) Retaliation against a whistleblower shall constitute an offence under this Act.

**48. Non-compliance by police.—** (1) Wilful failure by a police officer to acknowledge or act upon a mandatory alert without lawful cause shall constitute misconduct under applicable service laws.

(2) The Authority shall report patterns of police non-compliance to the Federal Government and provincial government concerned.

**49. Victim protection coordination.—** Where a case involves a victim requiring protection, the Portal shall notify the prescribed victim support agencies in accordance with the rules.

## CHAPTER VIII  
## CRIMINAL PENALTIES

**50. Operating an illegal jirga.—** Whoever convenes, participates in as a member, secretarially assists, or conducts any proceeding or records or enforces any decision through an illegal jirga shall be punishable with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to three years, or with fine which may extend to five hundred thousand rupees, or with both.

**51. Conducting jirga outside Portal.—** Whoever conducts or purports to conduct any jirga proceeding, or records or issues any jirga decision, otherwise than through the Portal shall be deemed to have operated an illegal jirga and shall be punishable under section 50.

**52. Facilitating prohibited matters.—** Whoever, through a registered jirga or illegal jirga, facilitates, orders, recommends, or enforces any matter prohibited under section 36 shall be punishable with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to seven years, or with fine, or with both.

**53. Swara and Vani.—** Whoever facilitates Swara or Vani through any jirga shall, without prejudice to punishment under the Prevention of Anti-Women Practices (Criminal Law Amendment) Act, 2011 (Act VIII of 2011), be punishable with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to seven years, or with fine which may extend to one million rupees, or with both.

**54. Concealment of criminal offence.—** Whoever knowingly conceals, omits to report, or deliberately misclassifies a cognizable offence before a jirga on the Portal shall be punishable with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to five years, or with fine which may extend to seven hundred fifty thousand rupees, or with both.

**55. Compelling withdrawal of complaint.—** Whoever compels or attempts to compel any person to withdraw a criminal complaint or refrain from reporting an offence to police in connection with a jirga matter shall be punishable with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to five years, or with fine, or with both.

**56. False Portal record.—** Whoever knowingly makes, files, or causes to be filed any false or misleading record on the Portal shall be punishable with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to three years, or with fine which may extend to five hundred thousand rupees, or with both.

**57. Obstruction of Authority or police.—** Whoever obstructs the Authority, a District Monitoring Officer, or a police officer in the performance of functions under this Act shall be punishable with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to two years, or with fine which may extend to three hundred thousand rupees, or with both.

**58. Retaliation against whistleblower or victim.—** Whoever retaliates against a person for reporting an illegal jirga, criminal matter, or rights violation under this Act shall be punishable with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to three years, or with fine, or with both.

**59. Unauthorized Portal access.—** Whoever gains or attempts to gain unauthorized access to the Portal or discloses protected Portal data shall be punishable with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to three years, or with fine, or with both.

**60. Abetment and conspiracy.—** Whoever abets or conspires to commit an offence under this Act shall be punished with the punishment provided for the principal offence.

**61. Corporate liability of jirga.—** Where an offence under this Act is committed by a registered jirga, every member who knowingly participated and the secretary shall be deemed guilty unless he proves that the offence was committed without his knowledge and that he exercised due diligence to prevent it.

**62. Cognizability and bail.—** (1) An offence under sections 52, 53, 54, and 55 shall be cognizable and non-bailable.

(2) Other offences under this Act shall be cognizable and bailable unless otherwise provided.

**63. Jurisdiction to try offences.—** (1) An offence under this Act shall be tried by a court of sessions.

(2) Notwithstanding anything contained in the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898 (Act V of 1898), it shall be lawful for the police to investigate offences under this Act in accordance with law.

**64. Complaint by Authority.—** The Authority may file a complaint or report in respect of any offence under this Act.

## CHAPTER IX  
## ADMINISTRATIVE PENALTIES

**65. Administrative fines.—** (1) The Authority or a District Monitoring Officer may, after notice and opportunity of hearing, impose on a registered jirga or member an administrative fine not exceeding five hundred thousand rupees for—

(a) late filing of case, proceedings summary, or decision record;

(b) breach of the Code of Conduct not amounting to a criminal offence;

(c) failure to renew registration;

(d) inaccurate but non-deliberate classification;

(e) failure to complete training; or

(f) non-compliance with a lawful direction of the Authority.

(2) An administrative fine shall be recoverable as arrears of land revenue.

**66. Suspension of registration.—** (1) The Authority or a District Monitoring Officer may suspend registration of a jirga where—

(a) there is prima facie evidence of a prohibited matter;

(b) decision recording was attempted while the case was locked;

(c) two or more administrative penalties were imposed within twelve months;

(d) a member was convicted or charged with a Schedule I offence; or

(e) obstruction of audit or inspection is established.

(2) Suspension may be interim or for a specified term not exceeding five years.

**67. Cancellation and debarment.—** (1) The Authority may cancel registration and permanently debar a jirga or member where there is repeated or grave non-compliance.

(2) A debarred person shall not be registered as a member for the period prescribed in the order.

**68. Performance score.—** (1) The Authority shall maintain a performance score for registered jirgas and members in the prescribed manner.

(2) Performance scores may affect renewal, public recognition, and intensity of monitoring.

**69. Recovery of costs.—** The Authority may recover prescribed investigation and audit costs from a jirga found in material breach of this Act.

## CHAPTER X  
## APPEALS

**70. Appeal against refusal of registration.—** An applicant aggrieved by refusal of registration may appeal under section 34.

**71. Appeal against administrative orders.—** (1) A registered jirga or member aggrieved by an order of administrative fine, suspension, cancellation, or debarment may appeal to the Appellate Bench of the Authority within thirty days.

(2) The Appellate Bench shall consist of not less than three members, including one member with legal background.

(3) The Appellate Bench shall decide the appeal within sixty days.

**72. Appeal by parties.—** (1) A party aggrieved by a decision recorded on the Portal may appeal to the District Monitoring Officer or prescribed Appellate Officer on grounds of—

(a) procedural irregularity;

(b) coercion or duress;

(c) decision on a prohibited matter;

(d) decision recorded while case was locked; or

(e) breach of vulnerability safeguards.

(2) An appeal shall be filed within thirty days of the decision unless the Appellate Officer extends time for sufficient cause.

(3) The Appellate Officer may stay enforcement of the decision where necessary to prevent irreparable harm.

**73. Further appeal.—** A person aggrieved by an order of the Appellate Officer on a party appeal may apply to the Appellate Bench of the Authority within thirty days on a question of law or jurisdiction.

**74. Rights of appeal to courts preserved.—** Nothing in this Chapter shall limit the right of any person to seek remedies before a civil court or by way of constitutional petition before a High Court.

**75. Effect of pending appeal.—** Unless stayed, filing of an appeal shall not automatically suspend a lawful decision that is not void *ab initio*.

## CHAPTER XI  
## OVERSIGHT AUTHORITY

**76. Establishment of Authority.—** (1) The Federal Government shall, by notification in the official Gazette, establish an Authority to be known as the **National Jirga Monitoring Authority**.

(2) The Authority shall be a body corporate, having perpetual succession and a common seal, with power to acquire, hold, and dispose of property and to sue and be sued in its name.

(3) The Authority shall function under the administrative control of the Ministry of Law and Justice.

**77. Composition.—** (1) The Authority shall consist of—

(a) a Chairperson, being a person who has been a Judge of a High Court or the Supreme Court of Pakistan;

(b) five members, including expertise in law, human rights, information technology, police administration, and Islamic jurisprudence; and

(c) four provincial directors.

(2) The Chairperson and members shall be appointed by the Federal Government on such terms and conditions as may be prescribed.

(3) The Chairperson shall hold office for a term of four years and shall not be removed except in the manner prescribed for removal of a public servant on grounds of misconduct or incapacity.

**78. Functions of Authority.—** (1) The Authority shall—

(a) administer and supervise the Portal;

(b) grant policy directions for registration, classification, and monitoring;

(c) prescribe the Code of Conduct and Schedules;

(d) appoint or designate District Monitoring Officers;

(e) conduct inspections, inquiries, and investigations;

(f) impose administrative penalties;

(g) refer criminal matters to police and prosecutors;

(h) maintain registration of members and jirgas;

(i) publish an annual report to Parliament; and

(j) perform such other functions as may be necessary for giving effect to this Act.

(2) The Authority shall be independent in the performance of its quasi-judicial functions.

**79. Powers of inspection.—** (1) The Authority or a District Monitoring Officer may inspect the records of a registered jirga on the Portal and may conduct field inspection in the prescribed manner.

(2) A person obstructing inspection shall be liable under section 57.

**80. District Monitoring Officer.—** (1) The Authority shall appoint a District Monitoring Officer for each district or for such combined districts as may be necessary.

(2) The District Monitoring Officer shall perform registration, audit, first-instance party appeals, and field oversight functions.

**81. Fund of Authority.—** (1) There shall be established a fund of the Authority to be administered by it.

(2) The fund shall consist of grants from the Federal Government, provincial contributions, fees, fines, and other lawful receipts.

**82. National Jirga Police Liaison Officer.—** (1) The provincial government shall designate at least one National Jirga Police Liaison Officer in each district for coordination with the Portal.

(2) The Liaison Officer shall acknowledge and facilitate police response to Portal alerts.

**83. Annual report to Parliament.—** The Authority shall submit an annual report on the administration of this Act to the Federal Government for laying before both Houses of Parliament.

**84. Power to issue directions.—** The Authority may issue binding directions to registered jirgas for compliance with this Act, subject to appeal under Chapter X.

**85. Delegation.—** The Authority may delegate any of its functions to the Chairperson, a member, a provincial director, or a District Monitoring Officer, except the power to make rules.

---

## CHAPTER XII  
## DIGITAL RECORD KEEPING

**86. Mandatory digital record keeping.—** (1) Every registered jirga shall create and maintain on the Portal a complete digital record of every case, proceeding, and decision.

(2) No oral-only record, private notebook, or off-Portal document shall satisfy the requirements of this Act.

**87. Proceedings summary.—** (1) The secretary shall file a proceedings summary on the Portal within twenty-four hours of each hearing or meeting.

(2) The proceedings summary shall record date, place, members present, parties present, and summary of deliberations.

**88. Decision record.—** (1) The secretary shall file a decision record on the Portal within forty-eight hours of the decision.

(2) A decision record shall include—

(a) case identification number and JRN;

(b) findings and directions;

(c) compliance timeline;

(d) digital signatures or authentications of the requisite quorum of members;

(e) party acknowledgment or recorded refusal; and

(f) notice of appeal rights.

(3) The Portal shall generate a cryptographic hash or other immutable audit marker for each decision record.

**89. Immutability and corrections.—** (1) A decision record once submitted shall not be edited or deleted.

(2) Corrections may be made only by supplementary filing with reasons and audit trail.

**90. Evidentiary value.—** (1) A decision record lawfully recorded on the Portal shall be *prima facie* evidence of the decision and of the members who authenticated it.

(2) A record of an illegal jirga shall not be admissible as proof of lawful settlement in any court.

**91. Retention.—** (1) Digital records shall be retained for such period as may be prescribed, but not less than ten years for civil matters and twenty-five years for matters involving criminal flags unless otherwise directed by a court.

(2) Destruction of records contrary to this section shall constitute an offence.

**92. Access to records.—** (1) Parties, the Authority, police, prosecutors, and courts shall have access to records in accordance with prescribed roles.

(2) Public access shall be limited to anonymized statistical data and registration verification.

---

## CHAPTER XIII  
## ANNUAL AUDITS

**93. Annual audit of registered jirgas.—** (1) Every registered jirga shall be subject to audit by the Authority or District Monitoring Officer at least once in every financial year.

(2) The audit shall examine compliance with registration, Portal use, classification, decision recording, police notification, and prohibited matters.

**94. Special audit.—** The Authority may order a special audit of any jirga where a complaint, whistleblower report, media report, or risk flag gives reasonable grounds of non-compliance.

**95. Audit report.—** (1) The auditor shall prepare an audit report on the Portal within thirty days of completion of audit.

(2) The report may recommend administrative penalties, suspension, criminal referral, or corrective directions.

**96. Compliance plan.—** A jirga receiving an adverse audit report shall submit a compliance plan within fifteen days, unless otherwise directed.

**97. Audit of Authority.—** (1) The accounts of the Authority shall be audited annually by the Auditor-General of Pakistan.

(2) A performance audit of the Authority may be undertaken by the Auditor-General or prescribed body every three years.

**98. Publication.—** The Authority may publish anonymized audit statistics in its annual report.

## CHAPTER XIV  
## MISCELLANEOUS

**99. Indemnity.—** No suit, prosecution, or other legal proceeding shall lie against the Federal Government, the Authority, the Chairperson, members, District Monitoring Officers, or any person acting in good faith under this Act for anything done or intended to be done in good faith in pursuance of this Act or the rules.

**100. Public servants.—** The Chairperson, members, provincial directors, and District Monitoring Officers shall be deemed public servants within the meaning of section 21 of the Pakistan Penal Code, 1860 (Act XLV of 1860).

**101. Bar of jurisdiction.—** No court shall grant an injunction or other relief against the Federal Government, the Authority, or a District Monitoring Officer in respect of any act done or intended to be done in pursuance of this Act, except as provided by law.

**102. Power to amend Schedules.—** The Federal Government may, on the recommendation of the Authority, by notification in the official Gazette, add to, omit from, or otherwise amend Schedule I or Schedule II.

**103. Power to make rules.—** (1) The Federal Government may, by notification in the official Gazette, make rules for carrying out the purposes of this Act.

(2) In particular and without prejudice to the generality of the foregoing power, such rules may provide for—

(a) registration, renewal, and tier criteria;

(b) Portal workflows, offline sync, and technical standards;

(c) case classification and risk scoring;

(d) police alert categories and response times;

(e) performance scoring;

(f) fees and waivers;

(g) training curricula;

(h) victim protection coordination;

(i) public dashboards; and

(j) any matter required to be prescribed.

(3) The power to make rules shall be subject to the condition of previous publication.

**104. Power to remove difficulties.—** If any difficulty arises in giving effect to any provision of this Act, the Federal Government may, by order in the official Gazette, make such provision not inconsistent with the provisions of this Act as may appear to be necessary for removing the difficulty:

*Provided that* no such order shall be made after the expiry of two years from the commencement of this Act.

**105. Federal-provincial coordination.—** (1) The Federal Government may enter into arrangements with provincial governments regarding police liaison, funding, and field implementation.

(2) Police and prosecution shall continue to function under provincial law, subject to mandatory Portal alerts under this Act.

**106. Act not to affect court jurisdiction.—** Nothing in this Act shall derogate from the jurisdiction or powers of any court, tribunal, or authority established under the Constitution or any law.

**107. Savings.—** (1) No act done before the commencement of this Act shall constitute an offence under this Act.

(2) Civil rights and remedies arising from unlawful jirga conduct before commencement shall remain unaffected.

**108. Repeal and transition.—** (1) The Federal Government may, by notification, prescribe a transition period not exceeding ninety days during which registration shall be opened and awareness campaigns conducted.

(2) Upon expiry of the transition period, sections 6, 7, 13, and 18 shall apply in full force.

### CODE OF CONDUCT — MINIMUM STANDARDS

1. Comply with the Constitution and all applicable laws.

2. Use the Portal for all proceedings and decisions.

3. Refuse to hear prohibited matters.

4. Report cognizable offences to police through the Portal immediately.

10. Protect privacy and confidentiality except as required by law.

**STATEMENT OF OBJECTS AND REASONS**

Traditional jirgas continue to operate throughout Pakistan without uniform registration or oversight. This has enabled decisions contrary to law, particularly affecting women and children, and has allowed criminal matters to be concealed from police. The present Bill creates a mandatory digital registration system, declares any jirga conducted outside the official Portal to be illegal, prohibits unlawful decisions, requires police reporting, establishes the National Jirga Monitoring Authority, and provides criminal and administrative penalties. The Bill does not abolish jirgas; it regulates them in the public interest and to secure fundamental rights.
