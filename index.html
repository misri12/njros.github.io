About NJROS

Regulating Community Dispute Resolution Without Abolishing It

NJROS establishes a statutory, digitally enabled regulatory framework for organized community dispute forums that issue written or binding decisions — while preserving the supremacy of courts and police.

The Government of Pakistan proposes NJROS to address a critical governance gap: unregulated organized community dispute forums — commonly termed jirgas, panchayats, faislo councils, and analogous bodies — have in documented cases facilitated unlawful settlements in matters involving sexual offences, homicide, forced marriage, Swara, Vani, inheritance deprivation, and coerced land transfer.

NJROS shall not regulate informal gatherings, social meetings, religious congregations, or political assemblies. It shall not create a parallel judicial system. Courts remain supreme; police retain exclusive criminal investigation powers.

The system comprises mandatory registration, a national digital platform, five independent case workflows, an independent oversight authority (NCDOA), and comprehensive rights and integrity frameworks aligned with the Constitution of Pakistan, 1973.

The Challenge

A Governance Gap with Documented Human Costs

The State presently lacks a unified registration, reporting, and oversight architecture for forums that purport to issue binding community decisions.

0

Unregistered Community Forums

Estimated operating forums across Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Balochistan, Punjab, Sindh, and merged districts with no national register.

0

Hidden Settlements on Record

No mandatory standardized record-keeping exists for binding community dispute outcomes affecting legal rights and property.

0

Systemic Deficits Identified

No national register, no standardized records, no criminal referral duty, no inspectorate, and no unified complaint channel.

0

Delayed Justice in Appeals

Current informal mechanisms lack statutory timelines for appeals, inspection findings, or independent oversight decisions.

Our Solution

Why NJROS?

NJROS offers regulation without abolition — transparency, accountability, and rights protection within the constitutional framework of Pakistan.

Core Capabilities

Built for Accountability and Citizen Protection

Transparency

Mandatory digital record-keeping for all registered forum proceedings and written outcomes. Anonymized public statistics enable evidence-based policy without compromising individual privacy.

Accountability

Independent oversight through NCDOA with registration, inspection, investigation, referral, and appeals powers. Annual reporting to Parliament and biennial Human Rights Impact Assessments.

Digital Records

Tamper-evident digital case files with audit trails, NADRA identity verification, and integration with Police Record and Statistics Management System (PSRMS) for incident cross-referencing.

Police Notifications

Standardized criminal incident protocol ensures allegations are referred to police within two hours for priority cases. No automatic FIR registration — police retain statutory investigation discretion.

Human Rights Protection

Safeguards for women, children, minorities, persons with disabilities, victims, and witnesses. Prohibition on private settlement of non-compoundable offences including Swara, Vani, and forced marriage.

Constitutional Compliance

Grounded in Articles 4, 8, 9, 10, 10A, 14, 25, 37, and 175 of the Constitution. Courts remain supreme; NJROS operates as a regulatory and record-keeping layer, not a parallel judiciary.

Case Workflows

Case Categories

Five independent workflows ensure each dispute type receives appropriate procedural safeguards and authority notifications.

Civil

Property, land, debt, and contractual disputes between parties within registered forums.

Family

Marriage, inheritance, custody, and maintenance mediation with mandatory female representation safeguards.

Community

Inter-community conflicts, boundary disputes, and collective grievances requiring elder mediation.

Administrative

Disputes involving local government, utility access, and public service delivery grievances.

Criminal Reports

Incident registration and police notification only — no adjudication, no automatic FIR, no private settlement of non-compoundable offences.

Institutional Design

Governance Framework

NJROS operates through cooperative federalism — federal legislation with provincial implementation agreements for police liaison and field administration.

Federal Government

Policy approval, legislative authorization, PSDP budget, and NCDOA establishment

Provincial Governments

Police coordination, prosecution, district administration, and provincial cost-sharing

District Administration

Field facilitation, local coordination, and District Registration Officer functions

Police

Exclusive criminal investigation powers; incident verification and statutory discretion on FIR registration

Oversight Authority (NCDOA)

Registration, inspection, investigation, referral, appeals, and national data governance

Community

Registered forum members, citizens, complainants, and whistleblowers participating in lawful dispute resolution

Constitutional Compliance

Courts remain supreme under Article 175; no parallel adjudicatory jurisdiction.

07

Privacy

Personal data protection with minimization, purpose limitation, and consent frameworks.

08

Cyber Security

Annual IT security audits per CERT/NITB standards with disaster recovery protocols.

Independent Oversight

NCDOA Board with retired judiciary chairperson and functional integrity officer.

Written findings, right to respond, and 60-day appeal timelines for adverse decisions.

Complaint channels, whistleblower protection, and public comment on draft regulations.

A phased five-year programme from institutional foundation through pilot districts to national consolidation.

Cabinet submission, inter-ministerial working group constitution, provincial consent for pilot districts, and CCI/IPCC Memorandum of Understanding template development.

Q3–Q4 2026

Policy Draft & Platform Build

Legislative drafting, NCDOA establishment, national digital platform development, NADRA API integration, and training curriculum approval.

Q4 2026 – Q2 2027

Phase 1 · Months 6–12

Cabinet Review

Federal Cabinet approval in principle, FPI Council constitution within 30 days, and authorization of five-year PSDP programme budget.

Q1 2027

Phase 1 · Months 8–14

Legislation

Parliamentary passage of the National Jirga Registration and Oversight Act, 2026. Provincial implementation agreements and rules publication.

Q2–Q3 2027

Phase 2 · Months 12–36

Pilot Programme

Six-district pilot in Swabi, Kohat, Loralai, Kech, Rajanpur, and Kashmore. Target: 800 registered forums and 5,000 cases filed in first 24 months.

Q3 2027 – Q2 2029

Phase 3–4 · Months 24–60

National Rollout

Provincial wave deployment, court ADR integration, external evaluation at Year 3 and Year 5, and HRIA update at 24 months post-commencement.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions About NJROS

Informal gatherings, social meetings, religious congregations, and political assemblies remain outside scope. The policy objective is regulation without abolition — adding transparency and accountability while respecting cultural legitimacy.
Absolutely not. NJROS does not confer adjudicatory jurisdiction parallel to courts. Under Article 175 of the Constitution, courts remain the supreme adjudicatory institutions. NJROS operates as a regulatory and record-keeping layer for registered community forums. Superior courts retain full power of judicial review, and constitutional petitions under Article 199 remain available at all times.
No. NJROS implements a criminal incident protocol that registers allegations and notifies police within two hours for priority cases. Police retain exclusive statutory discretion on whether to register an FIR and conduct investigation. The system does not adjudicate criminal matters, impose criminal punishment, or permit private settlement of non-compoundable offences.
The pilot programme covers six districts: Swabi and Kohat (Khyber Pakhtunkhwa), Loralai and Kech (Balochistan), Rajanpur (Punjab), and Kashmore (Sindh). These districts were selected based on jirga density, provincial consent, and governance consolidation priorities in merged districts. A 90-day provisional registration grace period applies during the pilot phase.
NJROS implements a comprehensive data governance framework with purpose limitation, data minimization, consent requirements, and role-based access control. Personal data is encrypted at rest and in transit. Public statistics are anonymized. The platform undergoes annual IT security audits per CERT/NITB standards, with a dedicated Privacy and Personal Data Protection Framework aligned with Article 14 of the Constitution.
The indicative five-year total programme cost is PKR 5.03 billion (Cabinet White Paper v2.0), comprising capital expenditure for technology infrastructure, operational expenditure for NCDOA and field operations, and contingency reserves. Funding is sourced through federal PSDP allocation with provincial cost-sharing arrangements (approximately 30%) and optional development partner grants for pilot activities only.
