# PART I — POLICY FOUNDATION

# 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY AND CABINET RECOMMENDATIONS

## 1.1 Purpose

The Government of Pakistan proposes to establish the **National Jirga Registration and Oversight System (NJROS)**—a statutory, digitally enabled regulatory framework for **organized community dispute resolution forums** that issue or intend to issue **written or binding decisions** affecting legal rights, property, inheritance, marriage, compensation, or legal obligations.

NJROS shall **not** regulate informal gatherings, social meetings, religious congregations, political assemblies, or private conversations. It shall **not** create a parallel judicial system. Courts shall remain supreme; police shall retain exclusive criminal investigation powers; and criminal justice shall remain governed exclusively by applicable Pakistani law.

## 1.2 Policy Problem

Unregulated organized community dispute forums—commonly termed jirgas, panchayats, faislo councils, and analogous bodies—have in documented cases:

- facilitated unlawful settlements in matters involving sexual offences, homicide, forced marriage, Swara, Vani, inheritance deprivation, and coerced land transfer;
- operated without public record, independent oversight, or effective accountability;
- created friction with formal justice institutions without lawful authority to displace them.

The State presently lacks a unified registration, reporting, and oversight architecture for forums that purport to issue binding community decisions.

## 1.3 Policy Solution

NJROS comprises:

| Component | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| **Mandatory registration** | Of organized forums meeting the statutory definition |
| **National digital platform** | For registration, case intake, record-keeping, and authority notification |
| **Five independent workflows** | Civil; family mediation; community mediation; administrative disputes; criminal allegations |
| **Criminal allegation protocol** | Incident registration and authority notification only—no adjudication, no automatic FIR |
| **Independent oversight authority** | Registration, inspection, investigation, referral, and appeals |
| **Rights and integrity frameworks** | Human rights safeguards, data protection, cybersecurity, anti-corruption, whistleblower protection |

## 1.4 Cabinet Recommendations

The Inter-Ministerial Working Group recommends that the Federal Cabinet:

1. **Approve NJROS in principle** as federal policy;
2. **Direct the Ministry of Law and Justice** to finalize legislation on the narrowed statutory scope set out in Chapter 6;
3. **Establish the National Community Dispute Oversight Authority (NCDOA)** as the statutory oversight body;
4. **Constitute a Federal–Provincial Implementation Council** through the Ministry of Inter-Provincial Coordination;
5. **Authorize a phased five-year programme budget** within PSDP and provincial cost-sharing arrangements;
6. **Mandate pilot implementation** in six districts before national scale-up;
7. **Require a Human Rights Impact Assessment update** at 24 months post-commencement.

# 2. CONSTITUTIONAL BASIS

## 2.1 Constitutional Framework

NJROS is grounded in the Constitution of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan, 1973, and operates to **give effect to**—not to limit—constitutional guarantees.

| Article | Relevance to NJROS |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Article 4** | Rule of law and lawful action by all authorities and persons |
| **Article 8** | Laws inconsistent with fundamental rights void; custom cannot override constitutional rights |
| **Article 9** | Security of person; prohibition on unlawful deprivation of life and liberty |
| **Article 10** | Safeguards against arrest and detention |
| **Article 10A** | Right to fair trial and due process before courts of law |
| **Article 14** | Dignity of person; privacy of home |
| **Article 19** | Freedom of speech; subject to law—supports whistleblower reporting |
| **Article 25** | Equality before law and equal protection of law |
| **Article 25A** | Right to education—relevant to child protection in dispute forums |
| **Article 35** | Protection of marriage and family within law |
| **Article 37** | State responsibility to promote social justice and eradicate social evils |
| **Article 175** | Judiciary established and conferred jurisdiction by Constitution—**courts remain supreme** |
| **Article 142** | Federal and provincial legislative competence—requires cooperative design |

## 2.2 Constitutional Compliance Statement

NJROS:

- **does not** confer adjudicatory jurisdiction parallel to courts;
- **does not** authorize any forum to try offences or impose criminal punishment;
- **does not** permit private settlement of non-compoundable offences;
- **does** promote access to lawful dispute resolution (Article 37);
- **does** strengthen accountability for actions affecting fundamental rights (Articles 9, 14, 25);
- **does** support transparency and record-keeping consistent with Article 19A (right to information) where data protection permits.

## 2.3 Eighteenth Amendment and Cooperative Federalism

Criminal law, criminal procedure, and police are **provincial subjects** (Entries 1 and 2, Concurrent Legislative List as adapted). NJROS shall therefore operate through:

- federal legislation establishing registration, oversight, and digital infrastructure;
- **provincial implementation agreements** on police liaison, prosecution coordination, and field administration;
- **no federal usurpation** of provincial police powers.

The Council of Common Interests or Inter-Provincial Coordination Committee shall approve a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) template before commencement.

## 2.4 Judicial Oversight

Superior courts retain full power of judicial review. NJROS records shall be admissible as evidence subject to law; they shall not oust court jurisdiction. Constitutional petitions under Article 199 remain available at all times.

# 3. BACKGROUND AND POLICY CONTEXT

## 3.1 Institutional Context

Organized community dispute resolution forums have operated for centuries in parts of Pakistan, particularly Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Balochistan, Punjab, Sindh, and merged districts. They have resolved civil and inter-community disputes with community acceptance.

Formal courts remain the constitutionally designated adjudicatory institutions. The policy challenge is not the existence of community mediation, but the **absence of regulation** where forums issue binding decisions affecting rights and, in some cases, purport to resolve criminal matters.

## 3.2 Policy and Legislative Landscape

Relevant instruments include the Pakistan Penal Code, 1860; Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898; Prevention of Anti-Women Practices (Criminal Law Amendment) Act, 2011; provincial Alternate Dispute Resolution legislation; Legal Aid and Justice Authority Act, 2020; and superior court jurisprudence holding that forums cannot displace criminal prosecution or statutory rights.

No existing statute establishes **national registration and oversight** of binding community dispute forums.

## 3.3 International Obligations

Pakistan's obligations under ICCPR, CEDAW, CRC, and CAT require that informal justice mechanisms comply with international human rights standards. NJROS provides a measurable domestic implementation pathway.

# 4. PROBLEM STATEMENT AND EVIDENCE BASE

## 4.1 Documented Harms

Judicial decisions, ombudsman findings, and civil society documentation establish recurring patterns:

| Category | Nature of harm |
|----------|----------------|
| Sexual offences | Pressure on survivors to withdraw complaints or enter coerced marriage |
| Homicide | Private diyat/sulh arrangements circumventing lawful prosecution |
| Swara/Vani | Compulsory marriage of women and girls as dispute settlement |
| Inheritance | Denial of statutory inheritance through male-only deliberation |
| Land | Coerced transfer or deprivation of occupancy |
| Governance | Absence of auditable record and accountability |

## 4.2 Systemic Deficits

1. No national register of forums issuing binding decisions  
2. No mandatory standardized record-keeping  
3. No statutory duty to refer criminal allegations to police  
4. No independent inspectorate with suspension and referral powers  
5. No unified citizen complaint and whistleblower channel  

## 4.3 Evidence Limitations

National statistics on forum activity are incomplete. NJROS shall generate authoritative administrative data. Baseline surveys in pilot districts shall precede impact claims.

# 5. POLICY OBJECTIVES AND EXPLICIT NON-OBJECTIVES

## 5.1 Objectives

| No. | Objective |
|-----|-----------|
| O-1 | Register organized community dispute forums meeting the statutory definition |
| O-2 | Require digital record-keeping for registered forum proceedings and written/binding outcomes |
| O-3 | Prohibit forums from deciding matters reserved to courts or police |
| O-4 | Ensure criminal allegations are referred for police verification through a standardized incident protocol |
| O-5 | Protect rights of women, children, minorities, persons with disabilities, victims, and witnesses |
| O-6 | Establish independent oversight, inspection, appeals, and accountability |
| O-7 | Provide anonymized public data for evidence-based policy |

## 5.2 Explicit Non-Objectives

NJROS shall **not**:

| Non-Objective | Rationale |
|---------------|-----------|
| Abolish lawful community dispute resolution | Cultural legitimacy and access to justice |
| Regulate informal gatherings | Overbreadth; constitutional and political risk |
| Create parallel courts | Article 175; rule of law |
| Issue judgments in criminal matters | Exclusive court jurisdiction |
| Register FIRs automatically | Police statutory discretion and investigation standards |
| Replace police investigation | Provincial police mandate |
| Impose automatic guilt or sanctions without due process | Articles 10 and 10A |
| Authorize surveillance of communities | Privacy and misuse risk |

# 6. SCOPE AND APPLICABILITY

## 6.1 Statutory Definition (Policy Draft)

**"Registered Community Dispute Forum" (RCDF)** means an organized body—by whatever name called, including jirga, panchayat, faislo council, or council of elders—that:

(a) is convened on a recurring or institutional basis for dispute resolution; **and**  
(b) intends to issue or does issue a **written record** or **binding decision** affecting legal rights, property, inheritance, marriage, compensation, or legal obligations.

## 6.2 Exclusions

The following are **outside scope**:

- informal family or neighbour discussions without binding decision;
- religious congregations without binding dispute adjudication;
- political party or trade union meetings (unless purporting to issue binding dispute decisions);
- court-annexed or statutory ADR under provincial ADR Acts;
- arbitration under the Arbitration Act, 1940, unless functioning substantively as an unregistered RCDF;
- fatwa or religious advisory opinions without binding legal effect.

## 6.3 Geographic Scope

Whole of Pakistan, with provincial implementation agreements for GB and AJK per constitutional arrangements.

## 6.4 Temporal Scope

90-day transition for registration after commencement; no retrospective criminal liability for pre-commencement conduct.

## 8.1 Methodology

This HRIA follows UN Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights adapted for public administration, OECD governance standards, and World Bank social safeguard frameworks.

## 8.2 Impact Matrix

- Integrity Officer: reports administratively to Chairperson, functionally independent  

## 9.4 Decision-Making Matrix

| Decision | Authority |
|----------|-----------|

| Suspension (interim) | NCDOA Regional Director |

| Criminal referral | NCDOA Integrity Officer or DRO |
| Platform policy | NCDOA with IT Ministry concurrence |

# 10. OVERSIGHT AND INSPECTION FRAMEWORK

## 10.1 Types of Oversight

| Type | Frequency | Trigger |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Routine inspection** | Annual per registered forum | Calendar |
| **Risk-based inspection** | As needed | Complaint, incident referral, anomaly |
| **Thematic audit** | Annual national | NCDOA plan |
| **Financial audit (NCDOA)** | Annual | Auditor-General |
| **IT security audit** | Annual + post-incident | CERT/NITB standards |
| **Independent external evaluation** | Year 3 and Year 5 | Cabinet directive |

## 10.2 Inspection Powers

Inspectors appointed under statute may:

- access platform records;
- conduct field visits with 48-hour notice (immediate where imminent harm);
- interview parties separately;
- seize non-digitized records not filed on platform;
- recommend suspension, referral, or compliance orders.

## 10.3 Inspection Safeguards

- Inspector identification and written mandate;
- privacy minimization in interview;
- female inspector available for women complainants on request;
- written findings within 15 working days;
- forum right to respond before adverse recommendation.

# 11. INDEPENDENT APPEALS MECHANISM

## 11.1 Appeals Architecture

```
Registration refusal ──► DRO Review Panel ──► NCDOA Appellate Tribunal
Party grievance ───────► Forum Internal (optional) ──► NCDOA Appellate Tribunal
Administrative sanction ► NCDOA Appellate Tribunal
Tribunal decision ─────► High Court (constitutional / certiorari)
```

## 11.2 NCDOA Appellate Tribunal

- **Composition:** 3 members (legally qualified chair + 2 members)  
- **Tenure:** 3 years  
- **Jurisdiction:** Registration; sanctions; party appeals on procedural unfairness, coercion, prohibited matter, off-platform decision  
- **Timeline:** Decision within 60 days  
- **Powers:** Affirm, set aside, remand, recommend prosecution, order status correction  

## 11.3 Judicial Oversight

Tribunal is administrative, not judicial. Article 199 jurisdiction of High Courts is expressly preserved. No ouster clause beyond lawful administrative act protection.

## 12.1 Rejection of Automatic Scoring

The prior proposal's automatic performance scoring is **replaced**. No algorithmic or points-based downgrade of forum status shall occur without **individualized due process**.

| Action | Authority | Grounds |
|--------|-----------|---------|

# 13. REGISTRATION FRAMEWORK

## 13.1 Registration Tiers

| Tier | Scope | Approving authority |
|------|-------|---------------------|
| Tier I | Single village / neighbourhood | DRO |
| Tier II | Tehsil / multi-village | Deputy Commissioner on DRO recommendation |
| Tier III | District / inter-community | Commissioner on NCDOA Regional Director recommendation |

## 13.2 Registration Requirements

- Application on National Platform  
- Member CNIC verification via NADRA API  
- Code of Conduct acceptance  
- Designated trained secretary  
- Jurisdiction declaration  
- Community endorsement (2 of: UC chair, women's organization, bar association member, DRO observer)  
- No member with conviction for Schedule offence  

## 13.3 Unregistered Binding Decision

A forum issuing a **written or binding decision** within the statutory definition **without registration** shall be subject to administrative and criminal consequences under legislation. Oral custom without binding record remains outside scope but unlawful acts disclosed remain prosecutable.

# 14. NATIONAL DIGITAL PLATFORM — GOVERNANCE ARCHITECTURE

## 14.1 Platform Purpose

The National Platform is a **government record and routing system**, not an adjudicatory or law enforcement system.

## 14.2 Platform Functions (Exclusive List)

1. Forum and member registration  
2. Case intake by workflow type  
3. Proceedings and outcome record storage  
4. Criminal allegation incident registration  
5. Authority notification routing  
6. Appeals and complaints filing  
7. Anonymized statistics publication  
8. Audit log maintenance  

## 14.3 Platform Non-Functions (Prohibited)

- Adjudication or "judgment" issuance in any matter  
- Automatic FIR registration  
- Automatic arrest or coercive order  
- Social credit or punitive algorithmic scoring  
- Surveillance beyond case-related data  

## 14.4 Hosting and Standards

- G-Cloud / NITB compliant hosting in Pakistan  
- ISO 27001-aligned controls  
- 99.5% uptime SLA  
- Offline capture with mandatory sync within 72 hours

# 15. WORKFLOW A: CIVIL DISPUTES

**Matters:** land (non-criminal), debt, contract, boundaries, water shares, commercial disputes.

| Stage | Action | Platform role |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| 1 | Party application or forum intake | Case opened — Workflow A |
| 2 | Notice to parties | SMS/registered post trigger |
| 3 | Mediation sessions | Proceedings summary filed per session |
| 4 | Written outcome (if any) | Outcome record filed |
| 5 | Non-compliance | Party referred to civil court |

**Lock conditions:** criminal allegation tag triggers transfer to Workflow E.

---

# 16. WORKFLOW B: FAMILY MEDIATION

**Matters:** maintenance, visitation (non-custody final), engagement disputes—**not** child custody final orders or divorce decrees.

| Prohibited on platform | Lawful platform role |
|------------------------|---------------------|
| Child custody final determination | Facilitate interim agreement for court submission |
| Forced marriage / Swara / Vani | Record prohibited matter alert |
| Divorce decree | Refer to arbitration council / court |

**Mandatory cooling-off:** 14 days for engagement disputes; 60 days for inheritance-related family claims.

---

# 17. WORKFLOW C: COMMUNITY MEDIATION

**Matters:** inter-family feuds (non-homicide), neighbourhood disputes, resource use (pasture, forest access) without criminal allegation.

**Feud escalation protocol:** if threat of violence logged, DRO and police district control room notified for preventive action—not forum adjudication.

---

# 18. WORKFLOW D: ADMINISTRATIVE DISPUTES

**Matters:** disputes between citizens and local government or public bodies **only where** forum has community mandate and matter is not reserved to administrative tribunal or court.

**Requirement:** written consent of both parties and acknowledgment that outcome is recommendatory unless subsequently endorsed by competent authority or court.

---

# 19. WORKFLOW E: CRIMINAL ALLEGATIONS

## 19.1 Paramount Principle

**The platform shall never issue a judgment, determination of guilt, or criminal sentence in any circumstance.**

## 19.2 Criminal Allegation Protocol

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  STEP 1: RECEIVE REPORT                                          │
│  Source: forum secretary, party, citizen, whistleblower, DRO     │
└────────────────────────────┬─────────────────────────────────────┘
                             ▼
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  STEP 2: RISK ASSESSMENT (structured checklist — not AI-only)    │
│  Human review by DRO within 4 hours for high-risk categories     │
└────────────────────────────┬─────────────────────────────────────┘
                             ▼
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  STEP 3: INCIDENT NUMBER ASSIGNED (NCDOA-INC-YYYY-NNNNNN)          │
│  Not an FIR. Not a criminal case number.                         │
└────────────────────────────┬─────────────────────────────────────┘
                             ▼
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  STEP 4: NOTIFY RELEVANT AUTHORITIES                             │
│  District Police (NPLO dashboard); Prosecution (copy);             │
│  Child/Women protection units if applicable; NCDOA Integrity     │
└────────────────────────────┬─────────────────────────────────────┘
                             ▼
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  STEP 5: POLICE VERIFICATION (exclusive police function)           │
│  Acknowledge within prescribed time; investigate per CrPC          │
└────────────────────────────┬─────────────────────────────────────┘
                             ▼
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  STEP 6: NORMAL CRIMINAL JUSTICE PROCESS                         │
│  FIR if lawful; prosecution; courts — outside platform             │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## 19.3 Forum Obligations Upon Criminal Allegation

- Cease any binding outcome process in the matter  
- File incident notification within 6 hours of awareness  
- Preserve proceedings record on platform  
- **No** pressure on parties to withdraw allegations  

## 19.4 Platform Lock

Workflow A–D cases **lock** upon incident registration until police upload **verification status** (FIR registered / no FIR — reasons / referred other agency). Lock prevents outcome recording; it does not create criminal adjudication.

## 19.5 No Automatic FIR

Police shall retain **exclusive discretion** on FIR registration subject to CrPC and superior court law. NCDOA shall monitor non-acknowledgement; it shall not compel FIR where law does not permit.

# 38. INTERNATIONAL BEST PRACTICES AND COMPARATIVE LESSONS

| Jurisdiction | Model | Lesson for Pakistan | Why Pakistan differs |
|--------------|-------|---------------------|----------------------|
| **Kenya** | Traditional Dispute Resolution Act 2012 — registration + constitutional compliance | Registration works when paired with explicit prohibitions | Pakistan requires stronger criminal referral and federal-provincial police coordination |
| **India** | Judicial invalidation of khap panchayats (*Shakti Vahini*) | Courts must remain supreme | Pakistan adds **preventive** administrative layer before litigation |
| **Indonesia** | Adat mapping, national law supremacy | Documentation improves policy | Pakistan adds digital incident protocol |
| **Rwanda** | Abunzi mediation (limited jurisdiction) | Clear jurisdiction caps essential | Pakistan scope narrower—no integration into judiciary |
| **South Africa** | Community courts (statutory) | Institutional clarity | Pakistan **rejects** community courts; regulates forums only |
| **United Kingdom** | Accredited mediation providers | Accreditation not abolition | Pakistan forums are customary not professional mediators |
| **Australia** | Family dispute resolution (FDR) before court | Segregated workflows | Pakistan adopts workflow separation including criminal incident track |
| **New Zealand** | Community law centres + restorative justice (court-referred) | Restorative justice requires court anchor | Pakistan criminal matters never adjudicated on platform |

**Conclusion:** Pakistan's model is **regulatory and record-keeping**, not court substitution or community court creation.

# 41. CONCLUSION AND CABINET DECISION SOUGHT

NJROS, as revised in this White Paper, establishes a **lawful, narrow, and auditable** framework for organized community dispute forums that issue binding decisions. It strengthens—rather than displaces—courts, police, and constitutional rights.

**Cabinet is invited to:**

1. Approve NJROS in principle;  
2. Approve renamed **National Community Dispute Oversight Authority**;  
3. Direct legislative drafting on narrowed scope;

6. Authorize six-district pilot upon enactment.
